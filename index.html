Symptoms of Basal Cell Carcinoma

Common signs of Basal Cell Carcinoma include:

  • A pearly or waxy bump on the skin.
  • A flat, flesh-colored or brown scar-like lesion.
  • A bleeding or scabbing sore that doesnâ€™t heal.
  • A shiny pink or red bump that grows over time.

Precautions & Prevention

Follow these preventive measures to reduce the risk of Basal Cell Carcinoma:

  • Use sunscreen with at least SPF 30 every day.
  • Avoid prolonged sun exposure, especially between 10 AM - 4 PM.
  • Wear protective clothing, hats, and sunglasses.
  • Regularly check your skin for unusual spots or changes.
  • Avoid tanning beds and artificial UV exposure.
